Vegetable Soup

Vegetable soup is a hearty and nourishing meal that is made by simmering a variety of vegetables in broth or stock. It is a healthy and versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste and dietary needs. Vegetable soup is often made with a base of onions, garlic, and tomatoes, as well as a variety of other vegetables such as carrots, celery, bell peppers, potatoes, and leafy greens. Legumes such as beans or lentils can also be added to the soup for added protein. Aromatics such as herbs and spices are often used to flavor the soup, and optional protein sources such as tofu, tempeh, or chicken can also be included. Vegetable soup is usually served hot and can be garnished with fresh herbs or a sprinkle of cheese. It can be enjoyed on its own or served with a side of bread or crackers.

prep time

20-30 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

Step 1

Choose your vegetables and any optional protein sources. Wash and chop them into bite-sized pieces.

Step 2

In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add diced onions and sauté until they are translucent.

Step 3

Add minced garlic and sauté for another minute.

Step 4

Add the chopped vegetables and protein (if using) to the pot. Sauté for a few minutes until they begin to soften.

Step 5

Pour in enough broth or stock to cover the vegetables. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.

Step 6

Add any desired herbs and spices.

Step 7

Let the soup simmer for 30 minutes to an hour, or until the vegetables are tender.

Step 8

Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh herbs or a sprinkle of cheese if desired.
