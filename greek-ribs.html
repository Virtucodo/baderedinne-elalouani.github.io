Greek Ribs

Greek ribs are a flavorful and hearty dish that is popular in Greek cuisine. They are typically made using lamb or beef ribs, which are seasoned with a mixture of herbs and spices and grilled or broiled until they are tender and cooked through. The ribs are often marinated in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, and paprika, which helps to tenderize the meat and infuse it with flavor. They may also be brushed with a glaze made from lemon juice, honey, and tomato sauce, which gives them a sweet and tangy finish. Greek ribs are usually served hot, with a side of vegetables or a salad. They can be enjoyed as a main course or as part of a larger meal.

prep time

15-30 min

cook time

20-30 min.

serving

3 servings

instructions

Step 1

Preheat your grill or broiler.

Step 2

In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, garlic, salt, oregano, thyme, black pepper, and paprika.

Step 3

Brush the ribs with the olive oil mixture, making sure to coat both sides.

Step 4

In a separate small bowl, mix together the lemon juice, chicken broth, honey, and tomato sauce.

Step 5

Place the ribs on the grill or under the broiler, and brush them with the lemon juice mixture.

Step 6

Grill or broil the ribs for about 20-30 minutes, turning them occasionally and brushing them with the lemon juice mixture, until they are tender and cooked through.

Step 7

Serve the ribs hot, with your choice of side dishes. Enjoy!

ingredients

2 lbs lamb or beef ribs

2 tbsp olive oil

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 tsp salt

1 tsp dried oregano

1 tsp dried thyme

1/2 tsp black pepper

1/4 tsp paprika

1/4 cup lemon juice

1/4 cup chicken broth

1/4 cup honey

1/4 cup tomato sauce

tools

Grill or broiler

Tongs or a spatula for flipping the ribs

Small bowl for mixing the marinade and glaze

Basting brush for applying the glaze

Cutting board and knife for trimming the ribs (if necessary)

Measuring cups and spoons for measuring the ingredients

Oven mitts or grill gloves for handling the hot ribs and grill
