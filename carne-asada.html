Carne Asada

Carne asada is a popular Mexican dish made with grilled or broiled beef, typically marinated in a mixture of citrus juice, spices, and herbs. The most common cut of beef used for carne asada is skirt steak, which is a thin, flavorful cut that is best cooked quickly over high heat. Other cuts of beef, such as flank steak or ribeye, can also be used. Carne asada is typically served hot, with a variety of accompaniments such as tortillas, beans, salsa, and guacamole. It can be enjoyed as a main course or used as a filling for tacos, burritos, and other Mexican dishes.

prep time

2h.

cook time

4-5 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

Step 1

In a small bowl, whisk together the garlic, olive oil, lime juice, salt, cumin, chili powder, black pepper, paprika, onion powder, oregano, and cayenne pepper (if using).

Step 2

Place the steak in a shallow dish or resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over the steak.

Step 3

Marinate the steak in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or up to 8 hours.

Step 4

Preheat the grill to high heat.

Step 5

Remove the steak from the marinade and discard the remaining marinade.

Step 6

Grill the steak for 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or longer for desired doneness.

Step 7

Remove the steak from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing against the grain.

Step 8

Serve the carne asada hot, with your choice of sides and toppings. Enjoy!

ingredients

2 lbs skirt steak

3 cloves garlic, minced

1/4 cup olive oil

1/4 cup lime juice

1 tsp salt

1 tsp ground cumin

1 tsp chili powder

1/2 tsp black pepper

1/2 tsp paprika

1/2 tsp onion powder

1/2 tsp dried oregano

1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)

tools

Grill or grill pan

Small bowl

Whisk

Shallow dish or resealable plastic bag

Spatula

Meat thermometer (optional)
